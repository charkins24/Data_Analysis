NWEA Growth Percentile Calculator 2014
                     
  Instructions

STEP 1: Your school's baseline data is available on Dashboard. To access the data, go to "Reports" and click on "Additional School-Level Reports." Choose your school and "Accountability" for Report Category and click "View Report." Your school's data is under the "School Quality Rating Policy" group name and includes the average pretest (EOY 2013) scores for each of your grade levels.

STEP 2: Using the "All Students" columns from your baseline data, complete the "All Students" section under the "NWEA Reading and Math Growth (All Students)" heading on the "All Students" tab. This section will help you figure out your grade-level and whole-school growth targets. Simply enter the number of students and average score for each grade from your baseline data and you will see targets for the 10th, 40th, 50th, 70th and 90th percentiles. You will also see the all grades targets associated with each of these percentiles. Enter an EOY 2014 scale score goal for each grade level to determine your spring percentile goal.

STEP 3: Repeat Step 2 for any priority groups you have in your baseline data under the priority group sections on the "Priority_Other_Groups" tab. These sections work the same as the "All Students" section, but because your priority groups may have different average EOY 2013 scores, the growth targets will be different. Remember to select the priority group at the top of each section. The growth norms are the same for all priority groups, but labelling each section will allow you to come back later and remember which priority group you entered. All priority groups at your school will be included in your SQRP calculation for next year. NOTE: Because the threshhold pecentiles for a school to receive SQRP points are lower for priority groups than for the "all students" group, the calculator sections for priority groups show different percentiles in the headers (i.e., "30th percentile" instead of "40th percentile") and threshholds are calculated accordingly. See the SQRP Handbook for additional details.

STEP 4: You may also repeat step 2 for any of the "other" student groups in your baseline data (e.g., White, Asian, Free/Reduced Lunch, etc.) in the "Other Student Groups" section on the "Prioirity_Other_Groups" tab. While these student groups will NOT be included as separate categories in your SQRP rating the way priority groups will be, they are included in the baseline data (with a corresponding section in the calculator) solely for school planning and goal-setting purposes.

STEP 5: Use the "NWEA Reading and Math Attainment (All Students)" section on the ""All Students" tab to project your school's NWEA attainment targets. These percentiles are also based on national norms, but since they are for attainment (as opposed to growth), they the same for all schools regardless of starting point. Simply enter the number of students for each grade level (you may want to use the same numbers from your baseline data for the sake of consistency) and project grade level averages for next year. This will give you your school's school-level attainment percentile.

Step 6 (Optional): Repeat step 5 for any of the students groups (priority or "Other") under the "NWEA Reading and Math Attainment" heading in the "Priority_Other_Groups" tab. NOTE: Only the "All Students" school-level attainment percentile will be used in your school's SQRP calculation. All the attainment sections in the "Priority_Other_Groups" are included solely for school planning and goal-setting purposeses. For additional details, see the SQRP Handbook.   

STEP 7: Save the calculator.  Consider including the date and the name of your school in the file name, in case you want to come back later and update your spring goals.
